# Morris Services — 10/10 Redesign Plan

**The operating system for home services**

**Date:** 2026-07-10  
**Ambition:** Not the best junk removal website in Missouri. The experience standard every American home-service company will copy.  
**Reference bar:** Apple · Tesla · Amazon · Airbnb · Uber · Stripe · Linear · Notion · Chick-fil-A · Costco · FedEx · Neighborly · SERVPRO · College HUNKS · 1-800-GOT-JUNK?

---

## 0. The reframe (read this first)

### What you have today
A clean prelaunch marketing shell wrapped around a serious operations platform. Honest. Competent. Local-service shaped.

### What you are building
**Morris OS** — a multi-brand home services company where:

- Customers experience one calm, magical relationship with *Morris*
- Each division (Hauling, Windows, Pressure Washing…) is a product line with its own craft identity
- Employees run their day from a phone like Uber drivers run theirs
- Managers run divisions like Stripe Dashboard runs money
- Owners see the whole empire like a Linear roadmap + Amazon ops cockpit
- Growth to new cities / brands does not create chaos — it clones a system

### The wrong question
> “How do we beat local junk competitors?”

### The right question
> “If Apple opened a home services company tomorrow, what would the first 30 seconds of the website feel like — and what would the 10th job feel like for the customer, the crew, and the owner?”

### Scoring rule for this document
- **10** = category-defining; other companies will copy it within 3 years
- **8** = excellent by industry standards — **still a failure against this ambition**
- **≤7** = must be redesigned, not polished

Current public experience is ~5.8 against a local bar. Against an Apple bar it is closer to **3/10**. This plan closes that gap.

## 1. First principles — stolen from the greats, rewritten for home services

| Company | Principle to steal | Morris translation |
|---------|--------------------|--------------------|
| **Apple** | One hero product moment; obsessive reduction; hardware+software as one | One primary action per screen. Booking feels like configuring a product, not filling a form. Brand is felt in silence and materials, not slogans. |
| **Tesla** | Direct-to-consumer; status from the product; OTA improvement | No middleman marketplace feel. The Morris app/site *is* the store. Every job improves the next estimate. |
| **Amazon** | Obsession with removing friction; “and then what?” | After booking: tracking, prep, receipt, reorder, multi-service. Never a dead end. |
| **Airbnb** | Trust through design + social proof + host identity | Crew identity cards, photo proof, reviews tied to real jobs, host-quality storytelling for owner/crew. |
| **Uber** | Live operational clarity; status as product | Map, ETA, crew photo, “on the way” as first-class UX — not a fake chip. |
| **Stripe** | Invisible complexity; documentation-grade clarity; dashboard beauty | Pricing, disposal, tax, invoices: hard ops made feel simple. Admin that CFOs respect. |
| **Linear** | Speed, keyboard, taste, issue→cycle clarity | Ops UI with taste. Jobs as issues. Divisions as teams. Zero visual noise. |
| **Notion** | Empty states that teach; modular systems | Empty company / empty job / empty career states that onboard, not apologize. |
| **Chick-fil-A** | Hospitality as operating system | Tone, courtesy, “my pleasure” equivalents in copy, training, and post-job follow-up. |
| **Costco** | Membership trust; clear value; no gimmicks | Transparent pricing philosophy. Membership for commercial accounts. |
| **FedEx** | Logistics poetry; tracking as brand | Hauling/disposal logistics become a visible craft: “where your junk went.” |
| **Neighborly** | Multi-brand portfolio with shared standards | Morris Services = the seal. Divisions = crafts. Shared Morris Standard. |
| **SERVPRO** | Ritualized process; “what happens next” | Every service has a named protocol customers can recite. |
| **College HUNKS** | Uniform + personality as marketing | Crew presence is brand media. |
| **GOT-JUNK?** | Productized promise (“full truck”) | Simple units of value customers understand instantly. |

### The Morris Doctrine (non-negotiable)

1. **One relationship.** Customer has a Morris account. Divisions are services inside it — like Apple products in one Apple ID.
2. **Proof over promises.** Every claim is backed by photo, protocol, or data.
3. **Status is sacred.** Customers always know what happens next. Employees always know what to do next. Managers always know what is broken.
4. **Taste is a feature.** If it looks like a generic SaaS template or a franchise junk site, it ships as a bug.
5. **Systems scale; heroes don’t.** Anything that only works because Brad remembers it is incomplete.
6. **Hospitality in the last mile.** The website books the job; the crew *is* the brand.
7. **Multi-brand from day one in architecture; single-brand in emotion until earned.**

## 2. The answers that define the product

### What would make customers immediately trust us?
- A **Morris Standard** page as crisp as Apple’s privacy page: background checks, training, photo documentation, disposal ethics, insurance (when true), guarantee language that is specific not vague
- **Named protocol** for every service (“The Morris Clear-Out”) with 5 visible steps
- **Crew card before arrival** — face, name, vehicle, star history from real jobs
- **Price that explains itself** — visual volume + line items + “why this number”
- **No dark patterns** — preview is preview; live is live; never fake scarcity

### What would make them excited to hire us?
- Booking that feels like **configuring a product** (Airbnb listing quality + Stripe Checkout calm)
- Instant **photo estimate** that feels magical (snap → range → book)
- A sense of **joining something premium** — burgundy craft, not orange chaos
- “Your space, restored” as the emotional promise — before/after as the hero medium

### What would make them tell their neighbors?
- A **shareable after-card**: before/after, weight diverted from landfill, donation summary, crew shout-out — designed like a Polaroid + receipt hybrid
- Surprise hospitality: water bottles, shoe covers, “we swept the driveway” photo
- A referral that feels like **inviting someone into a club**, not a coupon code buried in email

### What would make them come back for every service?
- One **Morris Home** dashboard: junk, windows, gutters, snow — same account, same trust, new craft
- **Seasonal orchestration**: “Your spring clear-out is due” / “First freeze — gutter check”
- Commercial **standing orders** with one tap reorder
- Membership: priority windows, locked-in rates, annual property care plan

### What would make employees love working here?
- Uber-grade **daily clarity**: one screen = today’s money, route, training due, pride metrics
- Instant pay visibility / tip path / leaderboard that isn’t demeaning
- Training that feels like **Duolingo for trades**, not PDF hell
- Uniform + truck that make them proud to be seen at the grocery store
- Career ladder that is visible in the app, not a poster in a break room

### What would make managers run entire divisions?
- A **Division OS**: capacity, margin, NPS, crew health, disposal variance — one command surface
- Playbooks as software: “Saturday surge,” “estate cleanout protocol,” “storm response”
- Hiring → onboarding → first solo job as a single pipeline with blockers visible
- Cross-division staffing without spreadsheet archaeology

### What would make franchise-level growth possible without chaos?
- **Cloneable city pack**: brand kit, pricing engine, disposal network, training library, launch checklist
- Multi-tenant Morris OS from day one (company_id is already there — productize it)
- Central “Morris Standard” audits; local craft identity
- No tribal knowledge: every SOP is in the product

### What would make investors see a national brand?
- Unit economics dashboard that looks like Stripe Atlas meets SaaS metrics: LTV, take rate on platform, brand attach rate across divisions, payback on crew
- Proof that software creates moat (estimate accuracy learning, disposal optimization, demand forecasting)
- Narrative: “We are building the Shopify of home services execution — not another Angi lead marketplace.”

## 12. Definition of Done — what “10/10” means measurably

A surface is **10** only if all are true:

1. A stranger feels desire in 5 seconds
2. A skeptic finds proof in 30 seconds
3. A busy parent can complete the primary action in under 90 seconds on mobile
4. No button violates the Button Law
5. No copy uses banned language
6. Empty states teach
7. Status is real or omitted
8. The experience still feels premium at 320px width
9. An employee can complete core field flow with wet gloves conceptually (giant targets, low precision)
10. A manager’s morning fits on one screen
11. Adding a new craft/city does not require inventing new UX patterns
12. Someone in another state would say: “Why doesn’t every service company work like this?”

If any fail, it is **not 10** — even if local competitors look worse.
